# Data Card — `formulations.parquet`

> ⚠️ **This dataset is fully SYNTHETIC.** It was generated by
> [`src/biopoly/data/generate.py`](src/biopoly/data/generate.py) from a transparent,
> physics-informed model in [`chemistry.py`](src/biopoly/data/chemistry.py). It contains
> **no company or proprietary data**, and no real measured samples.
> The anchor values are plausible mid-range figures from the public biopolymer literature,
> used only to give the demo learnable structure. Results are illustrative.

## Why this shape
The dataset mirrors a realistic materials-ML problem: a lab
with a few thousand records linking a **formulation** to a handful of **material properties**,
with the real prize being *inverse design* ("given a target spec, return the formulation").

**Inputs**
- Polymer matrix fractions over `{PLA, PHA, PBAT, PBS, TPS, PCL}` (`frac_*`), 1–3 bases per blend.
- Additive fractions over `{plasticizer, nucleating, compatibilizer, fibre, chain_extender}` (`add_*`).
- Processing: `process_temp_c`, `process_time_min`.
- `primary_polymer` (categorical) and `tensile_protocol` (categorical covariate).

**Outputs (targets)**
`tensile_strength_mpa`, `melt_flow_index_g10min`, `biodegradation_60d_pct`,
`water_absorption_pct`, `optical_clarity_pct`.

**Metadata**: `sample_id`, `date` (3-year span), `supplier_batch` (`S1`/`S2`).

## Neat-polymer anchors and literature ranges
Each figure below is the *neat-polymer* anchor used by the generator, with the broad
literature range it sits inside. Blends interpolate these with mixing rules + interaction
terms (immiscibility, plasticiser softening, fibre reinforcement, crystallinity, thermal dose).

| Polymer | Tensile (MPa) | Typical lit. range | Biodeg@60d | Water abs. | Clarity |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| PLA  | 55 | ~45–70 | low (~15%) | ~0.5–1% | high (clear) |
| PHA/PHB | 30 | ~20–40 | high (~80%) | ~0.5–1% | low (opaque) |
| PBAT | 12 | ~8–20 (soft) | ~60% | ~1–2% | translucent |
| PBS  | 35 | ~30–40 | ~55% | ~1% | medium |
| TPS  | 4  | ~2–6 | very high (~90%) | very high (~25%) | low |
| PCL  | 16 | ~10–25 | ~45% | ~1.5% | medium |

Grounding sources (general property ranges, not exact values):
- FDM 3D printing & properties of **PBS/PLA** blends — PMC10649279
- FDM 3D printing & properties of **PBAT/PLA** blends — PMC11054694
- **PLA–thermoplastic-starch** filament for FFF — ACS Sustainable Chem. Eng. (10.1021/acssuschemeng.0c09413)
- Commercial **PHA** filament for 3D printing — ScienceDirect S2213846325003098
- B2B guide to **PHA/PBAT/PLA/PBS/PHB** biodegradable polymers — torisegroup.com; ukhi.com bioplastic definitions

## Deliberate realism
- **Structured missingness (not-at-random).** `biodegradation_60d_pct` is missing ~24% —
  more often for slow-degrading (PLA-rich) samples where the 60-day test was abandoned
  ("data is missing for a reason"). `optical_clarity_pct` missing ~32% — mostly when the
  sample is visibly opaque (fillers / phase separation).
- **Test-protocol covariate.** `tensile_strength_mpa` is measured under ISO 527 or ASTM D638;
  ASTM reads slightly higher. `tensile_protocol` is provided as a feature so the model can
  account for the protocol rather than mistaking it for a real effect.
- **Measurement noise + ~2% outliers** on every property.
- **Supplier-purity drift.** From 2025-07 (`supplier_batch == "S2"`), PBS batches lose purity —
  lower tensile, higher melt flow. This is a genuine distribution shift for the drift monitor
  and the retraining story.
